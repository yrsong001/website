Yiran Song

PhD Student, Biostatistics, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill

Single-cell omics and ML for genomics.

Advised by Dr. Fei Zou and Dr. Li Qian.

I am a Doctoral student in Biostatistics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, advised by Dr. Fei Zou and Dr. Li Qian. My research focuses on single-cell and spatial omics, statistical methods, and machine learning for genomics and translational applications.

I hold a Master of Science in Biostatistics from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and a Bachelor of Engineering in Biological Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, with exchange at Waseda University, Tokyo. I am a Graduate Research Assistant in Li Qian’s lab at the McAllister Heart Institute, applying statistical and machine learning methods to single-cell multi-omics and spatial transcriptomics.

My research interests include:

  • Single-cell and spatial omics: gene regulatory networks, cell–cell communication, and cardiac development and aging.
  • Statistical methods: inverse sampling and complex survey data; computable phenotypes and EHR-based inference.
  • Machine learning: deep learning for imaging and molecular data; anomaly detection and classification in clinical and industrial settings.
